Official Constitution of Rotisserie League Baseball

Simplified Version

I. Object and Teams

Object: Assemble a lineup of National League baseball players whose cumulative statistics exceed those of all other teams.

Teams: 12 teams is the objective but we have 11 teams for 2026. (If playing with fewer, adjust the player pool so ~80% of available players are drafted).

II. Rosters and Position Eligibility

Roster Limits

  • Active Roster Limits: 26 players maximum (expands to 31 in September).
  • Reserve Roster Limits: 14 players maximum.
  • Combined Maximum: 40 players at any given time.

Standard Draft Roster (26 Players)

5 Outfielders, 2 Catchers, 1 Second Baseman, 1 Shortstop, 1 Middle Infielder (2B/SS), 1 First Baseman, 1 Third Baseman, 1 Corner Man (1B/3B), 1 batter, 1 Utility (any position, including P), and 11 Pitchers.

Position Eligibility

A player may be drafted at any position where he appeared in 20+ games the preceding season (or the position he played most frequently if he didn't reach 20 games anywhere). Once the season begins, a player gains eligibility at a new position after appearing there at least once.

III. Drafts and Salaries

1. Auction Draft

  • Conducted the first weekend after Opening Day.
  • Each team has a $294 salary cap to acquire 26 active players.
  • Bidding starts at $1 and proceeds in $1 increments.

2. Reserve Drafts (Split format)

  • First-Year Player Draft (FYPD): Held March 1st. Four rounds for eligible first-year international/drafted players. Salary: $10. Status: "ML".
  • Second Reserve Draft: Immediately follows the Auction. Max 7 rounds (Salaries by round: $15, $10, $5, $5, $2, $2, $2). Status: "2". 60-minute time limit.
  • Final Reserve Draft: Held the second Tuesday post-auction. Salary: $5. Status: "2".

IV. Fees and Prize Money

  • League Statistician: $52 is deducted from the prize pool annually ($2/week) for the League Statistician.
  • Transactions: $1 per trade, player activation, or demotion.
  • Waivers: $10 fee for claiming a player on waivers.
  • September Expansion: $5 for each player added to the active roster beyond the standard 25.
  • Prize Pairs: The final standings determines the order of selection for prize pairs. See the expanded rules for the table of the prize pairs.

V. Standings and Statistics

Categories

8 total categories

  • Offense: BA, HR, RBI, SB
  • Pitching: ERA, W, S, Ratio ((BB+H)/IP)
  • Points are awarded from 11 (first place) to 1 (last place) per category.

Minimum Requirements

  • Fenokee IP Requirement: Teams must pitch at least 1,000 innings. Failure drops the team to 1 point in ERA and Ratio.
  • Fenokee AB Requirement: Teams must reach 4,250 at-bats. Failure drops the team to 1 point in BA.

VI. Transactions: Trades, Demotions, and FAAB

Trades

Permitted until August 31. No trades are allowed between September 1 and October 15, or between Roster Freeze Day and Draft Day. Traded players retain their salary and contract status. Players traded out of the National League cannot be traded by their Rotisserie owner.

Active/Reserve Movement

Players can be promoted/demoted for a $1 fee, but any demoted player must be replaced by a qualified, active MLB player (unless resolving an over-roster situation from a trade).

Free Agent Acquisition Budget (FAAB)

  • Each team gets a $100 FAAB budget for the season.
  • No minimum bid required.
  • Bids are submitted by phone or email to the League Secretary; contingency bids are allowed.
  • Ties go to the team lower in the standings.
  • If you lose a player to the American League via MLB trade, your FAAB is increased by that player's salary.

VII. Waivers and September Expansion

Waivers

Unclaimed/dropped players go on waivers for one week. Claims are processed in inverse order of the standings. Cost is $10. Minor leaguers claimed on waivers can be placed directly on the reserve list.

September Expansion

After September 1, teams may expand their active roster from 26 up to a strict maximum of 31 players (a max of 6 additions). These players must come from the reserve roster.

VIII. Contracts and Off-Season Roster Rules

Option Year

A player held for two consecutive seasons at the same salary must be dealt with before his third season. He can be released, signed for an "option year" at the exact same salary (and must be released the following year), or signed to a Guaranteed Long-Term Contract.

Guaranteed Long-Term Contracts

The player's salary becomes his current salary + $5 for each additional year. If you cut this player later, you must pay a buyout of twice the remaining contract value or $10 (whichever is greater).

Winter Roster

By October 15, teams must submit a winter roster of exactly 26 players. A winter waiver period follows ($10 per claim).

Roster Protection (Freeze)

  • Deadline: The Friday before Opening Day at 6:00 p.m. Central.
  • Limits: Teams must protect between 7 and 16 players on their active roster.
  • Reserve Protections: Teams may protect up to 3 "N-designated" (rookie/never active) players.
  • FYPD (ML-designated) Protections: Based on the previous year's finish.
    • 1st–5th place: keep up to 2
    • 6th–9th place: keep up to 3
    • 10th–12th place: keep up to 7 (in any combination of ML and N players)
